MILE 01

Our Story & Vision

Why a research scientist opened a gym in a strip mall.

"Our path to good physical and mental health has many roads. Along those roads, the journey is worth taking if we have good friends to share a laugh."

Hi everyone — this is Helena, the owner of Get Fit by Many Roads. I opened a fitness and wellness studio that welcomes everyone, and I wanted to share why.

When I was 12, I came to the U.S. from Formosa (the island now called Taiwan) without knowing the alphabet. My father had been a minister; coming here meant he and my mom took low-wage jobs and never really learned English. I remember counting the burn marks on my dad's arms from his work as a hotel baker.

Learning a new language and culture wasn't easy, but teachers took me under their wing, and with my parents' support I finished college and graduate school. After 30 years in research labs, I wanted to do something more meaningful — while still keeping my job.

I've raised two teenagers and watched too many of their friends struggle with mental health, reaching for vaping, drugs, and video games as an escape. I wanted to offer an alternative. After 18 years working out in nearly every gym in the Rockville area, I took the leap — my first business ever — to open a space with the hope of helping teens and kids find a way out of self-destructive habits.

Before I was five, back in Taiwan, cysts grew on my neck and doctors couldn't explain why. Two years of surgeries and bitter Chinese herbal medicine followed, spent mostly recovering on a lawn chair. What finally helped? A teacher put me on the local swim team, with daily calisthenics and swim practice. I saw firsthand how movement, paired with a community of like-minded peers, can heal.

Our classes are for adults and teens, and we host community events — outdoor walks, hikes, swimming, lunch get-togethers, dancing. By building a community of active, compassionate adults, we give teens and kids a safe place to stay physically active, build friendships, and stay on a path toward better physical, mental, and spiritual well-being. Down the road, I hope to offer homework help, after-school snacks, and a path for teens to become certified coaches themselves.

— Helena Chang, Many Roads Community, LLC

Notice of Consumer Rights (Maryland Health Club Services Law)
  1. Our business's registration number with the State's Consumer Protection Division is 6120.
  2. We are not required to carry a performance bond under Maryland Health Club Services law, because we do not accept more than three months' payment in advance or charge initiation fees over $200.
  3. If our facility is closed for a month or more, you're entitled to your choice of a prorated refund.
  4. You have the right to cancel this agreement within 3 business days of receiving a copy of it. Cancellation must be in writing — by email, in person, or by certified/registered mail — and entitles you to a full refund of all monies paid.
